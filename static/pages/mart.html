US States Health Mart

How health burden and Medicare spend compare across all 50 states + DC

What this is
A US-wide, state-level public-health data mart — built by AI, on demand

This workspace turns three public-health feeds — CDC chronic-disease indicators, CMS Medicare payments, and US Census population — into one clean 51-row table (every US state + DC). Describe what you want to see in plain language; a model plans which measures to include, the deterministic engine builds the table, and a second model analyses the real numbers. Nothing but measure names ever leaves this machine.

What every mart answers: how does health burden relate to Medicare spending across states? You choose which CDC measures define "burden" (one, or several combined into a 0–100 index); the mart always compares them against Medicare spend per capita. So the burden-vs-spend view is the constant spine — what changes each time is which conditions make up the burden and where the states land.
  1. Browse what's available — check the sources and CDC measure topics below so you know what can be built.
  2. Describe your mart in Step 1 in plain language (e.g. "compare obesity and smoking to Medicare spend for a policy audience"). Press Enter to plan; Shift+Enter for a new line.
  3. Choose the measures the AI matched, then review & build in Step 2. Pick more than one measure and they're combined into a single burden index.
  4. Explore — the AI decides which charts and insights fit your specific mart, then narrates the findings. Ask follow-up questions in plain language.
  5. Every mart is saved as its own queryable table and appears under My marts to reopen later. Use Reset (top-right) to start fresh.
